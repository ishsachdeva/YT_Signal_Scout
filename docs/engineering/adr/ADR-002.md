# ADR-002: Separate Raw Analytics, Deterministic Metrics, and Signal Generation

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-21

**Owner:** Product & Engineering

---

# 1. Decision Summary

Adopt a layered analytics architecture that strictly separates raw data acquisition, deterministic metric calculation, business signal detection, and AI-generated narratives.

---

# 2. Context

YT Signal Scout analyzes YouTube channels to identify emerging creators and generate actionable insights.

The platform will progressively introduce deterministic analytics, business rules, AI-assisted reasoning, reporting, and additional data sources. Without clear architectural boundaries, calculation logic, business rules, and AI reasoning could become tightly coupled, making the system difficult to test, maintain, and extend.

This ADR establishes the long-term analytics architecture before implementation expands.

---

# 3. Problem Statement

How should analytics be structured so that deterministic calculations, business interpretation, and AI-generated explanations remain independent while allowing the platform to evolve without architectural refactoring?

---

# 4. Decision Drivers

- Maintainability
- Separation of concerns
- Deterministic computation
- Testability
- Extensibility
- Strong typing
- Explicit contracts
- Future scalability

---

# 5. Goals

- Keep raw YouTube data immutable.
- Isolate every metric into an independent calculator.
- Separate business rules from metric calculations.
- Prevent AI from performing deterministic calculations.
- Allow future metrics, signals, and AI capabilities to evolve independently.

---

# 6. Alternatives Considered

## Option A — Layered Analytics Pipeline (Chosen)

### Pros

- Clear separation of responsibilities
- Highly testable
- Easy to extend
- Deterministic calculations
- Supports future AI capabilities
- Clear architectural boundaries

### Cons

- Additional architectural layers
- Slightly higher implementation effort

---

## Option B — Single Analytics Service

All calculations, business rules, and orchestration occur within one service.

### Pros

- Fewer classes
- Faster initial development

### Cons

- Poor separation of concerns
- Difficult to test individual metrics
- Increased coupling over time
- Larger service class

---

## Option C — AI-Driven Analytics

Delegate deterministic metric calculations to an AI model.

### Pros

- Minimal deterministic code

### Cons

- Non-deterministic results
- Difficult to validate
- Poor reproducibility
- Unsuitable for business-critical calculations

---

# 7. Decision

Adopt the following analytics pipeline.

```text
YouTube API
        │
        ▼
ChannelAnalytics
(Immutable Raw Dataset)
        │
        ▼
Analytics Calculators
(Deterministic Metrics)
        │
        ▼
CalculatedChannelAnalytics
(Immutable Calculated Metrics)
        │
        ▼
Signal Engine
(Business Rules)
        │
        ▼
AI Narrative Engine
(Human-readable Insights)
```

Each layer owns a single responsibility and communicates only through explicit contracts.

---

# 8. Decision Rationale

Separating deterministic calculations from business interpretation creates a predictable, maintainable, and testable architecture.

Individual metrics become reusable building blocks that can support multiple downstream consumers including reporting, dashboards, APIs, signal generation, and AI narratives.

AI is intentionally positioned at the end of the pipeline to explain deterministic outputs rather than generate them.

This architecture minimizes coupling while allowing each layer to evolve independently.

# 10. Consequences

## Positive

- Metrics become independently testable.
- AI cannot influence deterministic calculations.
- Business rules remain isolated.
- New metrics can be added without modifying existing calculators.
- Multiple downstream systems can reuse calculated metrics.
- Future architecture remains modular and extensible.

## Negative

- Additional architectural layers.
- More classes and interfaces.

## Risks

Future contributors may bypass the intended pipeline by placing business logic inside calculators or deterministic calculations inside AI components.

Architectural reviews and code reviews should enforce the defined boundaries.

---

# 12. Security Impact

No direct security impact.

Separating deterministic calculations from AI-generated content reduces the risk of unintended manipulation of business metrics.

---

# 13. Performance Impact

Metric calculations remain deterministic and lightweight.

Independent calculators may be parallelized in future without changing architectural boundaries.

---

# 14. Cost Impact

Negligible infrastructure impact.

The additional abstraction slightly increases implementation effort while significantly reducing long-term maintenance costs.

---

# 15. Operational Impact

The analytics pipeline becomes easier to test, debug, monitor, and extend.

Failures can be isolated to individual calculators without affecting business logic or AI components.

---

# 16. Future Revisit Criteria

Revisit this decision if:

- Real-time streaming analytics are introduced.
- Distributed analytics processing becomes necessary.
- Data volume significantly exceeds the assumptions of the current architecture.
- AI begins producing deterministic business outputs.
- Architectural boundaries become insufficient for future product requirements.
