## Role

Channels are observable publishing entities that group repeated behaviour. They provide stronger
context than isolated videos and support inspection of cadence, formats, topic persistence,
performance distribution, creator scale, and content patterns.

## Evidence contributed

Channel evidence may include canonical video populations, public channel facts, eligible format
counts, upload behaviour, view distributions, subscriber-relative facts where valid, topic breadth,
pattern recurrence, and change over time. Every claim must respect acquisition completeness,
missing values, public-data limits, and observation timestamps.

## Discovery and selection

Discovery finds candidate channels through authorized sources within explicit market and query
contexts. Selection into an opportunity reference set is a later Product/research decision based on
relevance, independence, scale diversity, recency, format, language, region, and data quality.
Search rank or a high-performing video does not automatically make a channel a competitor.

Reference sets should include credible small creators, established context, counterexamples, and
adjacent creators when each has a stated role. Channels must not be labelled permanently; relevance
is opportunity-specific.

## Why channels are not the outcome

A channel can demonstrate that content exists and attracts attention, but it cannot alone establish
an unmet need, repeatability for new creators, persistence, production accessibility, or creator
fit. The platform should progress from channel facts to patterns and market evidence, then to an
Opportunity Candidate and governed validation.

## Evolution

The existing factual Channel Intelligence boundary is a foundation. Future work may add channel
history, content-pattern evidence, topic portfolios, reference-set roles, and longitudinal change.
Those additions require research and versioned definitions; they must not mutate historical facts.

## Scope and limitations

Channel Discovery is not channel endorsement, scraping authorization, competitor scoring, niche
classification, opportunity validation, or recommendation. Public data omits important audience,
retention, distribution, and business facts. Subscriber counts may be hidden or rounded.

## Future considerations

Validate discovery coverage, channel independence, multilingual relevance, organization/creator
identity, reference-set diversity, and longitudinal observation needs before expansion.
