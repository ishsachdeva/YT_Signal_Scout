## Why competitors are selected

Competitors are reference channels relevant to a defined Opportunity, Market, audience, and Creator
Profile. They help users understand existing supply, audience expectations, repeated patterns,
production demands, differentiation space, and plausible creator paths. Selection does not imply
hostility, equivalence, endorsement, or permission to copy.

## Reference-set roles

- **Small-creator references** test whether evidence exists without incumbent advantages.
- **Established references** show mature audience expectations and durable content territory.
- **Adjacent references** reveal transferable patterns or substitute audience needs.
- **Contrasting references** expose failed, weak, inaccessible, or contradictory examples.
- **Format references** clarify production and distribution differences.

Each reference should have an explicit role; one channel may occupy different roles for different
opportunities.

## Evidence diversity

A credible set should seek independence across creators, multiple eligible videos, scale ranges,
time, formats where relevant, language/region, and supporting versus opposing evidence. Many highly
similar channels can still represent one copied pattern or common external cause. Quantity does not
replace independence or data quality.

## Selection principles

Selection should bind relevance criteria, observation time, market context, acquisition provenance,
data quality, and known exclusions. Small creators matter because accessibility is a core question;
established creators matter as context, not proof that a beginner can reproduce outcomes. Users
should be able to inspect why a channel is present or absent.

## Future scoring

Future research may evaluate transparent factual dimensions for reference relevance or diversity.
No competitor score, weight, ranking, or threshold is approved. Any scoring proposal must separate
reference relevance from channel quality and opportunity confidence, and must pass Product decision
governance before implementation.

## Scope and limitations

Competitor Discovery is not surveillance, copying, creator judgment, an exhaustive market census,
or proof of demand. Public channel facts do not reveal private resources, distribution, audience
overlap, revenue, or strategy.

## Future considerations

Research independence, ownership networks, creator versus organization identity, similarity,
reference-set coverage, negative examples, market localization, and expiry/versioning.
