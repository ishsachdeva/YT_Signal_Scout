# Opportunity Confidence

## Purpose

Define the meaning, communication, and limits of confidence attached to an Opportunity.

## Product Knowledge Status

**Status: Hypothesis.** The prohibition against success-probability claims is accepted Product
policy; proposed evidence dimensions and presentation approaches remain unvalidated. No confidence
formula, score, label, model, or runtime capability exists.

## Core meaning

Opportunity Confidence measures confidence in the quality of supporting evidence for a bounded,
versioned Opportunity. It does **not** predict creator success, YPP eligibility, time to eligibility,
views, subscribers, revenue, or future market conditions. Strong evidence can support a proposition
while a creator experiment still fails; weak evidence can coexist with eventual success.

Confidence should answer: “How sufficient, consistent, diverse, recent, persistent, and applicable
is the evidence behind this proposition?” It should not answer: “What is my chance of succeeding?”

## Possible evidence dimensions

Future governed research may examine:

- **Cross-channel consistency:** whether independent channels provide concordant evidence.
- **Small-creator success evidence:** whether outcomes appear without established-channel advantages.
- **Recent momentum:** whether current observations support present relevance.
- **Repeatability:** whether multiple videos and content patterns recur rather than spike once.
- **Engagement quality:** whether authorized observable engagement provides relevant supporting facts.
- **Market persistence:** whether evidence survives suitable time windows and seasonal context.
- **Production feasibility:** whether observed patterns have understandable resource demands.
- **Monetization potential:** whether plausible mechanisms have evidence without forecasting revenue.
- **Competitive intensity:** whether supply conditions help or hinder a new experiment.
- **Creator accessibility:** whether the Opportunity can be contextualized to explicit profile needs.

These are research directions, not approved components, weights, formulas, or thresholds. A dimension
may support, oppose, be unavailable, or be inapplicable.

## Communication

Any future confidence presentation should show the Opportunity version, evidence freshness, sample
coverage and diversity, strongest supporting facts, material counterevidence, missing dimensions,
data-quality limitations, creator-fit separation, and what new evidence could change the assessment.
Labels should use plain language. Numeric presentation requires proof that users interpret it as
evidence quality rather than outcome probability.

## Prohibited interpretations

Confidence must never be described as “success probability,” “likelihood of going viral,” “chance of
reaching YPP,” guaranteed demand, creator quality, or financial return. It must not hide weak
dimensions through aggregation, imply precision unsupported by data, or compare unrelated markets
without approved methods.

## Scope

This document contains no mathematical formula, scoring rule, model, threshold, weighting,
calibration method, or implementation contract. “Confidence score” is a future presentation concept
whose actual form remains undecided.

## Future considerations

Research user interpretation, evidence sufficiency, dimension independence, contradiction handling,
calibration language, missing-data states, accessibility, and whether categorical profiles are more
trustworthy than a single number. Any adopted policy requires a Product decision and supporting ADR.
