# Niche Discovery

## Purpose

Define niche concepts and their role in identifying bounded opportunities.

## Product Knowledge Status

**Status: Hypothesis.** The distinctions are accepted vocabulary, but niche identification,
validation, mapping, and user-facing discovery remain unvalidated and unimplemented.

## Niche structure

A niche is a coherent audience need and content territory with recognizable supply and expectations.
It may be described through audience, problem, topics, formats, language, region, creator ecosystem,
and production conventions. A micro-niche narrows that intersection enough to expose more specific
needs and competitive conditions while still supporting repeated content.

## Concept distinctions

| Concept | Core question | Time dependence | Decision state |
|---|---|---|---|
| Topic | What is this content about? | May be timeless or current | Semantic evidence |
| Trend | What attention or behaviour is changing? | Essential | Lifecycle evidence |
| Niche | Which audience need and content territory persist? | Contextual | Market structure |
| Micro-Niche | Which narrower intersection is meaningfully distinct? | Contextual | Market structure |
| Opportunity | Is this bounded direction worth a feasible experiment given evidence? | Versioned and current | Validated Product proposition |

A topic may appear across niches. A trend may affect several topics or one format. A niche may exist
without being an opportunity because supply is saturated, evidence is weak, or production is
inaccessible. An opportunity may combine a persistent niche with a temporary trend but is not
synonymous with either.

## Examples

For “personal finance,” budgeting is a topic. Practical finance education for first-job earners is
a niche. Bilingual budget setup for Indian first-job earners using mobile apps is a micro-niche. A
rise in attention after a regulatory or app change is a trend. It becomes an Opportunity only if
multi-channel, current, repeatable, market, and creator-feasibility evidence supports testing a
clearly bounded proposition.

For “coffee,” grinder calibration is a topic; home espresso education is a niche; low-cost grinder
calibration for beginners is a micro-niche; a new machine launch may create a trend. None alone is a
recommendation.

## Discovery philosophy

Discovery should combine bottom-up evidence from videos/topics/patterns with top-down audience and
market hypotheses. Candidate boundaries must remain inspectable and revisable. The system should
retain ambiguous, overlapping, and negative evidence rather than forcing every item into one niche.

## Scope and limitations

This document does not define clustering, taxonomy, semantic models, demand formulas, saturation
thresholds, confidence, or recommendation policy. YouTube categories and keyword lists are useful
inputs but not authoritative niche definitions.

## Future considerations

Research taxonomy stability, overlap, multilingual equivalence, audience validation, market
boundaries, content supply, persistence, and human review before implementing niche inference.
