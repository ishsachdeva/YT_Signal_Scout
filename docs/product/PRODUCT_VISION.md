## Mission

Help aspiring creators make evidence-informed choices about what to create by identifying YouTube
opportunities whose supporting evidence is recent, diverse, repeatable, explainable, and relevant
to the creator's real constraints.

## Vision

YT Signal Scout will become a YouTube Opportunity Intelligence Platform: a trusted decision-support
system that connects market evidence with creator feasibility. It should help users move from a
broad ambition—such as reaching YouTube Partner Program eligibility—to a defensible opportunity,
a reference set, and an actionable content direction while making uncertainty visible.

The platform does not promise success or replace creative judgment. It reduces avoidable guesswork.
It distinguishes observable public evidence from interpretation and interpretation from a
recommendation. It should be possible to reproduce why an opportunity was presented.

## Target users

The primary user is a beginner or intermediate creator selecting or refining a YouTube direction.
Agencies and content studios use the same evidence at portfolio scale. Research analysts use the
platform to inspect methods, limitations, and market change. Detailed needs live in
[User Personas](USER_PERSONAS.md).

## Problems

Creators face too much generic advice and too little decision-quality market evidence. They cannot
easily tell whether visible success reflects a repeatable opening, an established brand, one viral
event, short-lived news, or inaccessible production resources. They also struggle to compare an
attractive market with their language, time, skill, budget, and publishing capacity.

The core problem is not “find small channels.” It is “find opportunities where multiple independent
facts support a repeatable, accessible path worth testing.” Channels, videos, topics, and trends are
evidence used to validate or invalidate that opportunity.

## Platform evolution

Channel Discovery is the first capability because channels and videos provide observable public
evidence. It is not the destination. The platform evolves through topic, niche, trend, competitor,
creator, and opportunity intelligence; later phases may support planning, creation, publishing, and
growth feedback while preserving the evidence boundary.

```text
Public evidence -> market structure -> opportunity evidence -> creator fit
                -> transparent recommendation -> observed outcomes -> learning
```

## Capabilities

Long-term capabilities include evidence acquisition, factual channel intelligence, topic and trend
research, niche mapping, reference-set construction, opportunity validation, creator profiling,
evidence-confidence communication, transparent recommendations, planning support, and outcome
monitoring. Each capability must have an explicit Product purpose and must not collapse facts,
research conclusions, or Product policy into one opaque score.

## Positioning

YT Signal Scout is decision intelligence, not a generic analytics dashboard, keyword-volume tool,
viral-video feed, creator marketplace, or guaranteed-growth system. Its differentiation is the
combination of cross-channel public evidence, repeatability, creator accessibility, explicit data
quality, and explanations that expose both supporting and contradictory facts.

## Success measures

Product success should eventually be evaluated across:

- decision quality: users can explain why they selected or rejected an opportunity;
- evidence quality: recommendations have sufficient, diverse, current, traceable support;
- trust: limitations and uncertainty are understood rather than hidden;
- usefulness: users progress from discovery to a feasible experiment;
- learning: outcome feedback improves research questions without rewriting history;
- responsible outcomes: the product does not overstate causality or likelihood of success.

Exact targets require separate Product decisions and validated instrumentation.

## Scope and non-goals

This vision covers opportunity decision support for YouTube creators. It does not guarantee YPP
eligibility, views, revenue, or a deadline; predict an individual creator's success; replace legal,
financial, or platform-policy advice; encourage copying; or treat public metrics as complete truth.
It does not authorize scraping, private-data use, autonomous publishing, or black-box recommendations.

## Future considerations

Future discovery should validate willingness to trust and act on evidence, accessibility concepts,
international and multilingual needs, outcome definitions, and how planning or publishing features
can reinforce rather than compromise the platform's decision integrity.
