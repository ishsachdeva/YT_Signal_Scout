# Product Roadmap

## Purpose

Describe the dependency-led evolution from research foundations to an Opportunity Intelligence
Platform. Phases communicate direction, not dates, scope commitments, or permission to implement.

## Product Knowledge Status

**Status: Vision.** The roadmap describes dependency-led direction. It contains no validated delivery
forecast, implementation approval, staffing commitment, or evidence that future phases exist.

## Roadmap principles

Evidence foundations precede recommendations; facts precede interpretation; market validity precedes
personalization; creator control precedes automation; and observed outcomes precede optimization.
Each phase must satisfy its own Product, research, security, and technical gates. Later vision must
not pressure earlier phases into placeholder algorithms.

## Scope

The roadmap provides long-term capability sequence and dependency context. It contains no dates,
staffing commitment, release acceptance criteria, or permission to implement a phase.

## Phase 1: Research Foundation

**Outcome:** Reproducible custody, labels, studies, evaluation, and decision governance.
**Existing foundation:** Immutable research artifacts and deterministic evaluation boundaries.
**Exit direction:** Research questions can produce traceable evidence without automatically changing
Product policy. Continue hardening quality and governance; no real study is implied by infrastructure.

## Phase 2: Channel Intelligence

**Outcome:** Reliable channel/video facts and diverse opportunity-specific reference sets.
**Capabilities:** Authorized acquisition, canonical populations, data quality, factual Channel
Intelligence, market-scoped discovery, and competitor/reference roles. **Exit direction:** Users and
researchers can understand why channels matter as evidence without receiving opportunity claims.

## Phase 3: Topic Intelligence

**Outcome:** Governed understanding of Topics, Content Patterns, Niches, Micro-Niches, and Trend
lifecycle. **Capabilities:** Source-aware topic evidence, multilingual research, pattern recurrence,
niche maps, and longitudinal context. **Exit direction:** Market hypotheses are interpretable and
validated against representative data; no opaque semantic classification is treated as truth.

## Phase 4: Opportunity Intelligence

**Outcome:** Opportunity Candidates can be validated, versioned, monitored, and explained.
**Capabilities:** Candidate registry, evidence assembly, opposing/missing evidence, lifecycle,
qualification policy, and Opportunity Confidence communication. **Exit direction:** A Product-approved
boundary distinguishes evidence-supported Opportunities from candidates without predicting success.

## Phase 5: Creator Intelligence

**Outcome:** Opportunities can be contextualized to explicit user goals and constraints.
**Capabilities:** Consent-based Creator Profiles, feasibility, explainable recommendations, reference
exploration, and bounded experiment planning. **Exit direction:** Users understand the evidence,
profile effects, uncertainty, and next reversible decision.

The v0.10.1 immutable Personal Creator Profile fact contract is an early foundation only. It does
not advance Opportunity contextualization, personalization, feasibility, onboarding, or
recommendations to Implemented status, and it does not change roadmap ordering.

## Phase 6: AI Content Planning

**Outcome:** Creators may receive assistive planning support grounded in selected Opportunities and
original contribution. **Capabilities:** Briefs, idea exploration, research organization, and planning
checks. **Prerequisites:** AI governance, provenance, evaluation, privacy, rights, safety, user review,
and non-deceptive design. AI remains assistive, not the source of market truth.

## Phase 7: AI Video Creation

**Outcome:** Selected production tasks may be assisted under creator direction. **Capabilities may
include:** draft assets, editing assistance, accessibility, and production adaptation. **Prerequisites:**
rights, consent, disclosure, model governance, safety, quality, platform-policy, and human approval.
No autonomous impersonation or unreviewed publishing.

## Phase 8: Publishing

**Outcome:** Users may move reviewed content through secure publishing workflows. **Capabilities:**
authorization, scheduling, metadata review, policy checks, audit history, and revocation. **Exit
direction:** Every external action is explicit, reversible where possible, least-privileged, and
observable. Opportunity evidence never grants automatic publishing authority.

## Phase 9: Growth Intelligence

**Outcome:** Observed outcomes help creators learn and opportunities be reassessed. **Capabilities:**
longitudinal observation, experiment review, portfolio learning, and evidence refresh. **Boundary:**
Outcome feedback must not create causal claims, reward harmful engagement tactics, or overwrite the
original recommendation/evidence record.

## Future considerations

Before scheduling any phase, validate its users, outcomes, dependencies, risks, operating model,
cost, and evidence maturity. Roadmap changes require the Decision Log and Feature Registry to remain
synchronized.
