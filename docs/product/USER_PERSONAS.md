# User Personas

## Purpose

Describe the users whose decisions the platform must support. Personas guide discovery and design;
they are not demographic stereotypes or authorization roles.

## Product Knowledge Status

**Status: Hypothesis.** These personas organize current assumptions and require user research. They
must not be treated as validated segments or evidence that corresponding workflows are implemented.

## Scope

Each persona includes goals, pain points, constraints, decision process, success measures, and
future opportunities. A real user may combine personas or change over time.

## Beginner Creator

**Goals:** Choose a credible direction, understand why it may work, reach initial platform
milestones, and develop a sustainable publishing habit. **Pain points:** Generic niche lists,
survivorship bias, information overload, unknown demand, and fear of wasting months. **Constraints:**
Limited budget, equipment, production experience, data literacy, audience, and weekly time.
**Decision process:** Starts with interests and constraints, examines accessible opportunities and
reference creators, tests a small content pattern, and reviews outcomes. **Success measures:** A
defensible choice, feasible first experiment, consistent production, clearer learning, and progress
toward self-selected goals—not guaranteed YPP attainment. **Future opportunities:** Guided
onboarding, explanations, experiment planning, skill-aware references, and learning loops.

## Intermediate Creator

**Goals:** Refine positioning, recover stalled growth, expand into adjacent topics, and choose among
several plausible directions. **Pain points:** Existing audience constraints, misleading dashboard
metrics, content fatigue, and uncertainty about whether a pattern transfers. **Constraints:** Brand
history, publishing cadence, team capacity, and risk of confusing current viewers. **Decision
process:** Compares current evidence with adjacent opportunities, inspects audience and format fit,
runs bounded series tests, and monitors persistence. **Success measures:** Better portfolio choices,
repeatable formats, useful audience response, and lower opportunity cost. **Future opportunities:**
Channel-aware baselines, adjacency maps, portfolio tracking, and experiment comparisons.

## Agency

**Goals:** Advise several clients consistently, identify market openings, and explain strategic
choices to stakeholders. **Pain points:** Manual research, inconsistent analyst methods, weak audit
trails, and pressure to overpromise. **Constraints:** Client-specific brand, budget, approval,
language, and reporting requirements. **Decision process:** Defines client context, constructs
reference sets, evaluates opportunities, records limitations, and presents alternatives. **Success
measures:** Reproducible research, client trust, decision speed, and clear rationale. **Future
opportunities:** Workspaces, governed templates, comparison views, exports, and role-aware reviews.

## Content Studio

**Goals:** Allocate production capacity across a portfolio, discover repeatable concepts, and match
opportunities to teams. **Pain points:** High cost of failed concepts, duplicated research, trend
chasing, and mismatch between ideas and production resources. **Constraints:** Talent availability,
format specialization, schedules, rights, and portfolio risk. **Decision process:** Screens a broad
candidate set, validates evidence diversity and persistence, assesses production fit, pilots, then
scales or stops. **Success measures:** More informative pilots, balanced portfolio risk, reusable
learning, and efficient capacity. **Future opportunities:** Portfolio evidence, resource matching,
scenario planning, and multi-market governance.

## Research Analyst

**Goals:** Produce defensible evidence, validate methods, identify bias and missingness, and inform
Product decisions without making them. **Pain points:** Mutable inputs, unclear definitions,
selection bias, undocumented policy, and pressure to interpret weak samples. **Constraints:** Public
data limitations, labelling cost, time windows, and uncertain ground truth. **Decision process:**
Defines a question, pre-declares method, validates custody and cohorts, executes deterministic
analysis, records limitations, and submits evidence for review. **Success measures:** Reproducible
artifacts, explicit uncertainty, independently reviewable conclusions, and Product traceability.
**Future opportunities:** Governed datasets, study tooling, evidence-quality audits, and bias review.

## Cross-persona needs

All personas need understandable terminology, visible provenance, explicit missingness, user control,
credible counterevidence, and a distinction between market evidence and promised outcomes. Advanced
workflows may expose more detail but must not weaken these trust requirements.

## Future considerations

Validate personas through interviews across regions, languages, creator categories, accessibility
needs, and business models. Add a persona only when it changes material decisions or workflows.
