# User Journeys

## Purpose

Describe end-to-end decisions the future platform should support without prescribing screens,
algorithms, or implementation.

## Product Knowledge Status

**Status: Vision.** These journeys describe desired future decision support. They are not validated
workflow knowledge and do not represent implemented screens, services, or automation.

## Journey principles

Journeys preserve user control, show why each input is requested, distinguish facts from policy,
allow “insufficient evidence,” and end with a reversible decision or learning step. A recommendation
is never the end of the feedback loop.

## Scope

These journeys describe Product outcomes and decision flow across current and future capabilities.
They do not prescribe screens, APIs, automation, algorithms, or committed delivery scope.

## New creator opportunity journey

```text
Goal and profile onboarding
  -> market/language selection
  -> opportunity candidate discovery
  -> evidence and limitation review
  -> competitor/reference exploration
  -> feasibility check
  -> bounded content experiment plan
  -> publishing outside or within future tools
  -> outcome observation
  -> profile and opportunity reassessment
```

The user can edit profile assumptions, reject suggestions, inspect counterexamples, and understand
that confidence concerns evidence quality rather than personal success. The desired outcome is a
well-reasoned experiment, not forced commitment.

## Existing creator expansion journey

The creator supplies current context and an expansion goal, reviews adjacent topics/niches and
possible audience overlap, examines whether reference patterns are accessible, selects a limited
series test, observes new and existing audience response, and decides whether to continue, adapt,
or stop. The journey protects against treating historical channel success as proof of transfer.

## Agency research journey

An analyst creates a client context, records market and profile constraints, discovers candidates,
constructs diverse reference sets, reviews evidence quality, annotates limitations, and prepares
several transparent options. Client feedback and approvals are retained in future workflow design.
The platform supports consistent evidence; it does not replace agency accountability.

## Studio portfolio journey

The studio establishes portfolio objectives and capacity, screens opportunities across markets,
checks overlap and correlated risk, matches candidates to production capabilities, runs multiple
bounded pilots, and allocates further resources only after outcome review. Future portfolio views
must not turn uncertainty into false-precision rankings.

## Research governance journey

```text
Research question -> pre-declared study -> immutable observations -> validation
  -> factual evaluation -> independent review -> Product decision consideration
  -> accepted/rejected/deferred policy -> traceable feature implication
```

Researchers produce evidence; Product owns user-facing interpretation and approval. Negative and
inconclusive results remain valuable records.

## Failure and recovery states

Every journey must handle incomplete acquisition, hidden subscribers, insufficient cross-channel
coverage, contradictory evidence, stale observations, inaccessible production demands, creator
profile mismatch, and changed goals. Recovery may request more evidence, broaden or narrow context,
offer an alternative, or return no recommendation. It must never silently relax standards.

## Future considerations

Validate sequencing, abandonment points, explanation needs, consent, accessibility, collaboration,
and outcome feedback through user research before UI design or automation.
