## Name

High median subscriber-relative reach.

## Purpose

Identify a channel whose typical eligible standard video reaches materially more views than its
current public subscriber scale, subject to an approved comparison policy.

## Business value

Help creators find channels with sustained subscriber-relative reach that may deserve closer
attention. The signal describes public performance facts; it does not predict future success.

## User problem solved

“Which qualified channels have typical eligible-video reach that materially exceeds their current
public subscriber count?”

## Category

`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`. The `discovery.*` machine identity is catalog-defined, but ADR-006
defers a broader category taxonomy.

## Dependencies

- Eligible Video Policy v1 and canonical standard-video classification.
- Subscriber-relative qualification under ADR-010.
- `eligible_standard_video_count` and `median_standard_video_vsr` factual analytics.
- Policy-free `SignalEvidenceBundle` under ADR-011.
- Approved threshold research and product policy decision.
- A catalog-approved emitted-evidence extension and subscriber-relative rule-input boundary.

## Analytics required

- `eligible_standard_video_count: int`, standard-video scoped, where zero is factual.
- `median_standard_video_vsr: float | None`, ratio scoped to eligible standard videos; `None`
  indicates an unavailable valid calculation.
- Visible positive subscriber state and shared acquisition/evaluation provenance.

Absolute average views, views per day, upload-cohort growth, and a single-video outlier are not
substitutes for median subscriber-relative reach.

## Qualification requirements

The existing `SubscriberRelativeQualification` must be qualified. This requires uploads-playlist
provenance for the same channel, complete pagination, at least 60% requested-ID resolution, visible
positive subscribers, and at least five eligible standard videos. Any qualification failure emits
no SIG-002 signal. Missing, hidden, or zero subscribers are not numeric zero VSR.

## Formula

Approved analytics fact:

```text
video_vsr_i = eligible_standard_video_i.view_count / public_subscriber_count
M = median(video_vsr_i)
```

Proposed business condition from the catalog:

```text
emit SIG-002 when qualified and M [PENDING PRODUCT DECISION] T
```

No rounding occurs before comparison.

## Units

- `M` and `T`: dimensionless views-per-current-public-subscriber ratio.
- `N`: eligible standard-video count.
- Resolution: dimensionless ratio.
- Evaluation and source times: timezone-aware timestamps.

## Comparator

`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`. The catalog proposes `>=`; it is not approved production behavior.

## Threshold

`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`. Product and Analytics must approve `T`, provenance, validation evidence,
and affected rule version.

## Boundary conditions

- Unqualified analysis: no signal.
- Unavailable `M`: no signal.
- Fewer than five eligible standard videos: qualification failure; no signal.
- Hidden, missing, or zero subscribers: qualification failure; no signal.
- Non-finite or negative VSR: invalid upstream structure, not a business non-match.
- Comparison uses full stored precision without pre-rounding.
- Duplicate emission from one rule invocation is prohibited by the intended zero-or-one contract.

## Equality behavior

`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`. The catalog proposes that equality triggers, but approval is required.

## Expected inputs

An immutable qualified `SubscriberRelativeAnalysisResult` or an architecture-approved rule input
that preserves its complete qualification and evidence context. Bare subscriber-relative analytics
must not bypass qualification. `CalculatedChannelAnalytics` does not contain SIG-002 inputs.

## Expected outputs

Intended cardinality is zero or one immutable positive `Signal` with the identities and version in
this specification. Final cardinality/coexistence approval remains tied to the production policy.
No severity, confidence, score, generated timestamp, or human narrative belongs in the current
`Signal` contract.

## Evidence required

- Observed `M` and ratio unit.
- Approved threshold `T` and comparator.
- Eligible sample count `N` and standard-video basis.
- Qualified status and qualification policy version.
- Visible-positive subscriber state.
- Source channel, snapshot/evaluation timestamp, and acquisition provenance.
- Rule ID and version through the containing signal.

## Evidence structure

ADR-011 already provides policy-free qualification, sample, subscriber state, requested-ID
resolution, median VSR, unit, availability, timestamp, and provenance facts. Current emitted
`SignalEvidence` lacks comparator, threshold, semantic unit, sample size, eligibility basis, and
complete subscriber-relative provenance. The smallest rule-specific extension is
`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION` plus Architecture approval. Generic dictionaries are prohibited.

## False positives

- Current rounded subscriber counts can inflate ratios for small channels.
- Older eligible videos may have accumulated views over materially different exposure periods.
- External traffic or one-time promotion can raise views without durable audience response.
- Conservative format classification can change the represented sample.

## False negatives

- Recent videos may not have accumulated representative views.
- Hidden or unavailable subscriber counts prevent valid evaluation.
- Conservative format exclusions can omit relevant videos.
- Incomplete acquisition or failed qualification suppresses emission intentionally.

## Known limitations

The denominator is current public subscribers, not subscribers at publication time. Public counts
may be rounded. Median reduces single-hit sensitivity but does not prove growth, conversion,
causality, virality, or future performance. The policy observes only eligible public standard
videos within the governed acquisition snapshot.

## Examples that SHOULD trigger

`PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`. Numeric examples require approved `T`, comparator, and equality policy.

## Examples that MUST NOT trigger

- Any unqualified analysis.
- Any analysis with unavailable median VSR.
- Any input that bypasses subscriber-relative qualification.
- Numeric boundary examples: `PENDING PRODUCT DECISION`.

## Future improvements

Consider exposure-age context, historical denominators, or cohort context only through separately
approved facts and rule versions. Do not silently convert this signal into ranking or prediction.

## Performance considerations

Rule evaluation should be constant-time over precomputed analytics/evidence. It must not reclassify
videos, recalculate VSR, call YouTube, access persistence, or perform research execution.
